Why This Matters

Most websites look fine until trust breaks.

A website does not need to be hacked to hurt your business. Weak trust signals, exposed admin areas, poor security headers, broken forms, missing policies, and visible technical mistakes can make customers hesitate before they ever contact you.

01

Lost Trust

Visitors judge credibility fast. If the site feels outdated, exposed, or poorly maintained, they may leave before becoming a lead.

02

Visible Weakness

Public-facing issues such as missing HTTPS controls, weak headers, exposed paths, and unclear contact flows can create unnecessary business risk.

03

No Clear Roadmap

Many owners know something is wrong but do not know what to fix first. The report turns technical problems into business priorities.

Report Preview

What the final report looks like.

The goal is not to overwhelm you with technical language. The goal is to show what matters, why it matters, and what to fix first.

Sample Finding

Missing Security Headers

Medium Severity

Business Impact

Missing browser security controls may increase exposure to client-side attacks, reduce trust, and make the website appear less professionally maintained.

Evidence

Header review shows missing or incomplete protections such as Content-Security-Policy, X-Frame-Options, and other browser-side controls.

Recommended Fix

Add recommended security headers through the hosting provider, CDN, or web server configuration. Re-test after implementation.

Priority

Fix within 30 days unless the website handles sensitive customer data, payments, or account logins.
