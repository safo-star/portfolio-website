Report Preview

What the final client report looks like.

The report is designed to be useful for a business owner: issue, evidence, impact, priority, and fix.

Sample Finding

Phishing and Email Risk

High Severity

Business Impact

Staff may trust a fake sender, click a suspicious link, or open a risky attachment.

Evidence

Sender mismatch, suspicious language, short links, or failed authentication signals.

Recommended Fix

Improve email authentication, staff awareness, suspicious email reporting, and verification steps.

Priority

Review immediately if payment, passwords, customer data, or admin accounts are involved.

Sample Finding

Cloud Account Risk

Critical Severity

Business Impact

Cloud account compromise can expose files, customer data, admin settings, and billing resources.

Evidence

Failed login bursts, impossible travel, MFA disabled, admin role changes, or large downloads.

Recommended Fix

Enforce MFA, review admin roles, rotate keys, restrict access, and monitor suspicious sign-ins.

Priority

Immediate if admin accounts, customer records, API keys, or storage buckets are involved.

Sample Finding

Suspicious Login Alert

Critical Severity

Business Impact

Repeated failed logins can mean someone is trying to guess passwords or break into an account.

Evidence

Multiple failed login attempts from the same source, followed by alert escalation and incident reporting.

Recommended Fix

Block suspicious sources, enforce MFA, review login logs, and reset exposed or targeted account passwords.

Priority

Immediate if admin accounts, payment systems, customer data, or employee accounts are involved.

Sample Finding

AI Chatbot Manipulation Risk

High Severity

Business Impact

An attacker may try to manipulate a chatbot into ignoring business rules, revealing hidden instructions, or producing unsafe responses.

Evidence

Prompt injection attempts, jailbreak language, role manipulation, system prompt extraction attempts, or unsafe data requests.

Recommended Fix

Add input filtering, stronger system prompts, safer response handling, logging, and review rules for suspicious AI interactions.

Priority

High if the chatbot handles leads, customers, bookings, payments, support, internal documents, or sensitive business data.

FAQ

Clear expectations before any work starts.

Is this a penetration test?

No. These are passive security readiness reviews unless a separate written scope is approved. No exploitation or unauthorized access is performed.

Do you need passwords?

No. Most packages are designed to work without passwords. If a deeper review is needed, permission and scope must be written first.

What do I receive?

A clear report with findings, risk ratings, business impact, screenshots/evidence where useful, recommended fixes, and optional alert setup notes.
